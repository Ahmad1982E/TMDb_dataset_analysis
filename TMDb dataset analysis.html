Introduction¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue .let's have some fun!

Dataset contains:¶

-10866 Rows. -20 cloumns.
Cloumns Headers¶

-id (A unique identification for each movie.)

-imdb_id (A unique identification for each movie on IMDBP).

-popularity (A numirec).

-budget (The budget spent in US Dollars).

-revenue (The earning amount in US Dollars).

-original_title (The released movie title).

-cast (Main actors for each movies).

-homepage (The link of the web site of movie if any).

-director (Movie's tagline).

-keywords (The keywords related to the movie).

-overview (a brief statement of descripe the story of the movie).

-runtime (The total duration of the movie in minutes).

-genres (the genre of the movie).

-production_companies (The production company of the movie).

-release_date (the date of releasing the movie).

-vote_count (The number of the votes that given to the movie).

-vote_average (The average score of rating).

-release_year (The movie released year).

-budget_adj (The budget of the movies in terms of exchange rate of 2010 US Dollars).

-revenue_adj (The revenue of the movies in terms of exchange rate of 2010 US Dollars).

Conclusions¶

1-The number of movies released has increased over time.

2-In 2014, there were most movies released.

3-1969 had the least number of movies released.

4-Approximately 3K movies have been released between 2011 and 2015.

5-AVATAR is the most profitable movie.

6-Here are the movies with the highest profit margins: -Avatar. -Star Wars: The Force Awakens. -Titanic. -Jurassic World. -Furious 7.

7-Drama, Comedy, Thriller, Action, and Romance were the most popular genres.

8-Adventure, Action, Comedy, Drama, and Thriller are genres that generate the most profit.

9-The relationship between the release year of the films and their revenue is left-skewed, implying that there is a correlation between them.

10-The majority of popular films last between 50 and 150 minutes.

We can dig deeper into the analysis process for some parts of our processes, such as the annual profits and the most popular and highly rated directors as example.

Limitation¶

In spite of the dataset is quite large, there are zeros and missed values that I have removed or substituted by the mean, which affects the final results in a negative way.

Depending on vote average is not quite accurate as long as the count of the votes of each movie varies from one to another.

As noticed there are some outliers in the datatset which might affect the accuracy of the results.
